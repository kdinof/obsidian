### üß† **Common Heuristics** (Mental Shortcuts)

These are **automatic decision-making rules** your brain uses to speed up thinking:

1. **Availability Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí If it comes to mind easily, it must be common or true.  
    _Example: Thinking plane crashes are frequent after seeing one in the news._
    
2. **Representativeness Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí If it fits a stereotype, it must be true.  
    _Example: Assuming someone with glasses is smart._
    
3. **Anchoring Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Relying too heavily on the first piece of information seen.  
    _Example: A product that ‚Äúwas $200, now $99‚Äù seems like a better deal._
    
4. **Affect Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Letting emotions drive judgments.  
    _Example: Buying something because it ‚Äúfeels right.‚Äù_
    
5. **Recognition Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Choosing the option you‚Äôve heard of before.  
    _Example: Trusting a brand just because it‚Äôs familiar._
    
6. **Social Proof Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Doing what others are doing.  
    _Example: Choosing a restaurant because it‚Äôs crowded._
    
7. **Authority Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Trusting someone in a position of authority automatically.  
    _Example: Believing a doctor even without evidence._
    
8. **Simplicity (Cognitive Ease) Heuristic**  
    ‚Üí Preferring things that are easy to understand.  
    _Example: Trusting a product more because the language is simple._
